Cybersecurity Audit Checklist for Personal Security Company

Key Considerations

Privacy: Ensuring that sensitive client information and movements remain confidential.

Communication Security: Securing communication channels between the personal security team and the client.

Threat Monitoring: Real-time monitoring for digital threats (e.g., hacking, social engineering) and physical threats.

Compliance: Following data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA) and ensuring no breaches that could lead to reputational or legal damage.

  • 1. Client Data Privacy
    • Ensure client data (location, personal information, schedules) is encrypted at rest and in transit.
    • Restrict access to client data on a need-to-know basis (role-based access control).
    • Utilize anonymization and pseudonymization techniques when managing high-profile client data.
  • 2. Secure Communication Channels
    • Ensure end-to-end encryption for all communication channels (e.g., mobile devices, emails, radios).
    • Regularly update secure messaging platforms and communication apps.
    • Implement VPNs and encrypted connections for remote communication between clients and the security team.
  • 3. Physical-Digital Security Integration
    • Ensure surveillance cameras and other physical security systems are secure and patched.
    • Use encrypted storage for video feeds and ensure that access is strictly controlled.
    • Secure access to client residence or business premises with biometric and multi-factor authentication.
  • 4. Threat Detection and Monitoring
    • Deploy threat monitoring systems to track cyber threats targeting high-profile clients (e.g., doxxing, hacking attempts).
    • Implement social media monitoring for potential threats against clients.
    • Set up alerts for abnormal digital activity or physical security breaches.
  • 5. Mobile Device Security
    • Ensure mobile devices used by clients and security teams are encrypted.
    • Use Mobile Device Management (MDM) solutions to manage, track, and wipe devices remotely if compromised.
    • Regularly update device software and remove unused apps that may introduce vulnerabilities.
  • 6. Incident Response and Recovery
    • Develop an incident response plan specifically for high-profile client data breaches or security compromises.
    • Train the security team on proper incident response procedures for both digital and physical threats.
    • Ensure immediate recovery measures for any compromised digital infrastructure (e.g., data restoration, forensic analysis).
  • 7. Employee Security and Access Control
    • Conduct thorough background checks for all employees with access to sensitive client information.
    • Implement strict access controls for physical and digital environments, ensuring no unauthorized access.
    • Provide regular cybersecurity awareness training to employees to recognize social engineering attempts and phishing attacks.
  • 8. Social Engineering and Phishing Defense
    • Regularly test staff with simulated phishing attempts to ensure vigilance.
    • Ensure that the security team and clients are trained on avoiding social engineering attacks.
    • Monitor for attempts to impersonate high-profile clients or their security teams.
  • 9. Compliance with Privacy Regulations
    • Ensure full compliance with data protection laws such as GDPR, CCPA, or other relevant local regulations.
    • Perform regular audits of data handling processes to avoid regulatory penalties.
    • Maintain comprehensive records of data access logs for legal and compliance purposes.
  • 10. Regular Security Audits
    • Conduct regular internal and external security audits to identify potential vulnerabilities.
    • Review access controls and employee permissions quarterly to ensure up-to-date security policies.
    • Update security protocols and infrastructure based on audit findings and emerging threats.
